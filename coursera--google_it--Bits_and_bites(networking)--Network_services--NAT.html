NAT


NAT takes one IP address translate it to another.(NAT is not a strickly define protocol rather a general techonology concept)
IP masquerading :What NAT is doing in this example is hiding the IP of computer one from computer two.
One to many NAT: All of the network IPs being translated by the router to its own. So, To the outside world, the entire address space of network A is protected and invisible.This is known as one to many NAT.
Port forwarding : Port forwarding is a technique where a specific destination ports can be configured to always be delivered to specific nodes.
Port preservation: A technique where the souce port chosen by a client is the same port used by the router.
